Hawaii: Sugar Cane and landlessness

Sugar companies have indelibly marked our bodies, our government, and our planet.

Around the world, they've exacerbated landlessness among the most marginalized people, they’ve polluted our groundwater, and they’ve worsened deforestation.

In America, they're responsible for misinforming parents deciding what to feed their children.

They've created America’s aberrant sugar consumption that exposes our children to cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, and obesities.

How do we change this?

We should become more aware of the food we put in our body, and actively reduce how much sugar we eat.

We should support public policy changes taxing sugar companies for the health externalities they create.

We should fight to keep sugary beverages vending machines out of schools.

We should shine a light on the sugar lobby’s hold on politicians around the globe, including our own.

While we face one of the biggest public health cresses of a generation, through collective action and individual responsibility we can secure a healthier future for our communities.
